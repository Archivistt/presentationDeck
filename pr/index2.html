Introduction & Background

Student Welfare Context: Nutrition significantly impacts cognitive function and academic performance; ensuring reliable access to meals is critical.

LVCC Program: La Verdad Christian College provides free meals to scholars, formerly managed through a manual coupon system.

Operational Challenges: The manual process caused significant inefficiencies, including long queues, data errors, and high administrative workload.

Technological Solution: Introduction of "Eat's on Tap"—a web-based RFID system to automate eligibility verification and meal claiming.

"The transition from manual coupons to RFID represents a critical shift toward operational efficiency in student welfare services."

By digitizing the process, the institution moves from reactive manual logging to proactive real-time management.
