Introduction & Background

Student Welfare Context: Nutrition significantly impacts cognitive function and academic performance; ensuring reliable access to meals is critical.

LVCC Program: La Verdad Christian College provides free meals to scholars, formerly managed through a manual coupon system.

Operational Challenges: The manual process caused significant inefficiencies, including long queues, data errors, and high administrative workload.

Technological Solution: Introduction of "Eat's on Tap"—a web-based RFID system to automate eligibility verification and meal claiming.

"The transition from manual coupons to RFID represents a critical shift toward operational efficiency in student welfare services."

By digitizing the process, the institution moves from reactive manual logging to proactive real-time management.

Problem Statement

Excessive Administrative Workload

The PSAS Department staff dedicates approximately 8 hours daily solely to physically sorting, organizing, and manually recording coupon submissions one by one.

Operational Bottlenecks

Manual verification creates long queues during peak meal times, reducing the time students have to eat and rest.

Resource Wastage

Recurring costs for vellum paper and laminating film, exacerbated by frequent issues of missing or misplaced physical coupons.

Security & Unauthorized Claims

Lack of effective validation mechanisms makes the system vulnerable to unauthorized meal claiming and identity misrepresentation.

Compromised Data Integrity

Manual record-keeping results in fragmented records and human errors, making accurate auditing and reporting nearly impossible.

Research Objectives

Zero Missing Coupons

Digitize meal credits to completely eliminate the issue of lost, misplaced, or physically damaged coupons.

Eliminate Physical Costs

Remove recurring expenses for vellum paper and laminating film used in the manual production of meal coupons.

Reduce Administrative Burden

Significantly cut down the 8-hour daily manual workload of PSAS Department staff for sorting and recording.

Prevent Unauthorized Claims

Use RFID UID-to-account linking to enforce strict one-student-one-meal accountability and identity verification.

Centralized Real-Time Monitoring

Provide PSAS staff with a comprehensive live dashboard for tracking meal claims, credit balances, and usage statistics in real-time.

Theoretical & Technical Framework

RFID Technology

Contactless Radio Frequency Identification enables fast, accurate, and touchless student verification at the point of service.

MERN Stack

Utilizes MongoDB, Express.js, React.js, and Node.js to provide a robust, scalable full-stack web development environment.

ISO/IEC 25010

International software quality model used to strictly evaluate characteristics such as performance efficiency, security, and usability.

Technology Acceptance Model (TAM)

A theoretical framework used to assess user acceptance by measuring Perceived Ease of Use and Perceived Utility.

Framework Integration

This study synergizes these pillars into a cohesive solution:

RFID handles hardware-level identity verification, the MERN Stack powers the web system, ISO/IEC 25010 sets the quality benchmarks, and TAM validates user adoption.

System Design & Architecture

MERN Stack Core

Utilizes React.js for the dynamic frontend, Node.js/Express.js for the robust API layer, and MongoDB for scalable real-time read/write data operations.

RFID Integration

Implements a "Tap-to-Verify" mechanism that instantly links each student's physical RFID card UID to their specific meal credit account.

Data Integrity

Maintains immutable, auditable transaction logs that record every single tap event, ensuring 100% accountability and accurate reporting.

Role-Based Access Control

Enforces strict security with distinct access levels and dashboards for PSAS administrators (monitoring) and canteen operators (service).

Discussion

Low-Latency RFID Validated

Fast contactless scanning was confirmed to reduce queue bottlenecks and waiting times significantly compared to manual logging.

Tap-to-Verify Exception Handling

The system clearly communicates error states (e.g., insufficient credits, unregistered UID) at the point of service without confusion.

One-to-One UID Linking

Each RFID card is uniquely bound to a student account, enforcing strict accountability and preventing unauthorized meal claims.

Framework Alignment

Findings are consistent with both ISO/IEC 25010 quality benchmarks (performance) and TAM adoption constructs (ease of use).

"The results confirm that RFID-based automation is not just technically viable, but operationally practical for institutional welfare programs."

Conclusion & Recommendations

Conclusion

Technically Superior: "Eat's on Tap" provides a robust, contactless alternative to the manual coupon distribution method.

Objectives Met: Achieved zero missing coupons, eliminated physical paper costs, and significantly reduced administrative burden.

Standard Compliance: System architecture and performance align with ISO/IEC 25010 quality standards and TAM acceptance model.

High User Approval: Validated by a UAT composite mean of 3.70 (Strongly Agree), confirming system quality and ease of use.

Recommendations

Phased Rollout: Implement a gradual campus-wide deployment accompanied by comprehensive staff training sessions.

Continuous Monitoring: Establish protocols for ongoing monitoring of system performance, security, and data integrity.

Periodic Reviews: Conduct regular usability reviews and system audits to identify areas for iterative improvement.

Advanced Analytics: Integrate deeper analytics and reporting features to assist the PSAS Department in resource planning.
